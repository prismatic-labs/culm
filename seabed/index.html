Deep-sea mining and the metals behind AI power

Seven licensed or explored sites, the metals they target, and who may control them next.

AI doesn't only run on chips. It runs on power, and power runs on metal. Copper wires and cools the datacenters; cobalt and nickel go into the batteries and grid that keep them on. Some of the largest known deposits under exploration sit on the ocean floor, where seven sites are already licensed or being explored. Copper does most of the work, and depth is the problem. The metals AI leans on most lie four or five kilometres down, deep enough that only a handful of operators can reach them, and no one yet knows what it really costs to bring them up. Tap a site on the map or the depth scale to see who holds it, which metals it targets, and how deep it goes. Every link is confidence-rated, with sources in each panel.
